CITY MANAGER
------------

"City Manager" is a city simulation game. Key aspects of the game are:

 - The city administrator (the player) manages every aspect of the city,
   except for the houses of the inhabitants.
 - In order to build the structures of the city (roads, industries, public
   buildings, etc) the administrator doesn't use money, but construction
   material. If the construction material are not available in the city, he
   can then buy from neighbour cities for a price. As such, money is not so
   important as for other city building games.
 - The industry is a important aspect of the game, where raw materials are
   fabricated and used to build more refined products. Every product that is
   not available must be purchased from the neighbours.
 - The quality of the houses of the inhabitants are dependant of the resources
   available around (schools, hospitals, jobs, etc) and of the salary of the
   people.
 - Traffic is a very important aspect, for during the time that the person is
   stuck in traffic, it is neither resting nor producing.
 - People's actions are governed by their needs, much like the Maslow pyramid.
